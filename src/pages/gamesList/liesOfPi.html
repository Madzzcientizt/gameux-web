        Overview

        Lies of P is a 2023 action RPG developed by Round8 Studio and published by Neowiz Games. Set in a dark, diesel-punk reimagining of the Pinocchio fairy tale, players control P — a human-like puppet navigating the crumbling fictional city of Krat, ravaged by the Petrification Disease and a catastrophic puppet uprising.

        Lies of P closely follows the Soulslike formula but distinguishes itself through its weapon assembly system — combining blades and handles from different weapons — the Legion Arm prosthetic tool, and a narrative choice system where lying or telling the truth shapes the ending. Its HCI borrows from Elden Ring and Bloodborne while introducing novel defensive mechanics centered on the Perfect Guard.

        UI Components

        [ ELEMENT_01 ] Perfect Guard System

        Lies of P's defensive core is the Perfect Guard — a precise parry mechanic activated by blocking at the exact moment of enemy contact. A subtle audio cue and flash indicate the guard window. Successful Perfect Guards restore weapon durability, integrating defense into weapon management as a dual-purpose mechanic.

          Guard System Design
        • Temporal Audio Cue Parry Window
        • Durability Recovery on Perfect Success
        • Guard Regain HP Chip Mechanic
        [ ELEMENT_02 ] Weapon Assembly Interface

        Players combine weapon blades and handles sourced from different weapons — a Krat Police Baton blade on an Electric Coil Stick handle. The crafting table UI presents stat previews and elemental affinities for each combination, enabling deep build-crafting through a physical weapon combination metaphor.

        [ ELEMENT_03 ] Legion Arm & P-Organ Tree

        The Legion Arm replaces the Ash of War system with a selectable prosthetic limb tool — Fulminis lightning, Puppet String grapple. The P-Organ upgrade tree is a branching node graph connecting skill nodes to passive bonuses, visualizing character progression as a biological mechanical system.

        Fitt's Law in Precision Guard-Based Combat

        In Lies of P, Perfect Guard timing is a narrow temporal W applied to every enemy attack. Unlike Elden Ring's roll-focused defense, the emphasis on parrying creates a system where players must continuously assess attack telegraphs and fire precise input inside a sub-300ms window — every attack is a Fitts's Law test.

        [ MATHEMATICAL MODEL ]
        MT = a + b * log2(2D/W)

        Boss Phase 2 transitions in Lies of P dramatically reduce the Perfect Guard W — attack animation speeds increase, forcing players to recalibrate their parry timing. Same D (reaction to attack initiation), reduced W (tighter input window): a direct temporal Fitts's Law difficulty escalation.

        User Interaction

        An examination of Lies of P's soulslike HCI refinements — studying how Round8 Studio iterated on established Soulsborne conventions with novel guard mechanics and modular weapon design to create a distinctly calibrated action RPG interaction model.

        Core Gameplay Loop

        1. Observe

          P reads enemy attack telegraphs — wind-up animations, footstep patterns, and audio cues — to anticipate incoming strikes and prepare Perfect Guard timing before the attack window opens.

        2. Guard & Riposte

          The Perfect Guard replaces rolling as the primary defensive tool. A successful guard opens a Fable Art riposte window — a high-damage follow-up that depletes enemy posture and rewards the precision risk.

        3. Attack & Break Posture

          Sustained guard breaks and weapon strikes deplete enemy Stagger Gauges — triggering a Fatal Attack cinematic execution prompt when fully depleted, rewarding the patient posture-breaking strategy.

        4. Upgrade & Lie

          Between areas, P visits Hotel Krat to level up, reassemble weapons at the workbench, and make narrative dialogue choices — deciding whether to lie or tell the truth, shaping P's humanity score and ending.

        Multi-Modal Feedback Matrix

        Lies of P's feedback design prioritizes precision and punishment — every sensory cue is calibrated to reward mastery of the Perfect Guard while making failures viscerally and immediately unambiguous.

        • Audio Feedback

          A metallic clang distinguishes Perfect Guards from standard blocks. Fury Attacks — unblockable enemy strikes — are preceded by a red glow and deep audio boom, providing multi-sensory pre-warning of an unblockable input requirement.

        • Visual Feedback

          Perfect Guard flashes produce a distinctive white spark ring. Stagger-break animations briefly freeze time, framing Fatal Attack prompts with dramatic weight that rewards the player's patience and precision investment.

        • Haptic Feedback

          Pulse-based rumble escalates with enemy attack impact. Perfect Guard triggers a sharp short burst distinct from a regular block — providing tactile confirmation of the precise timing within the narrow guard window.

        HCI Principles

        Cybernetic Feedback Loops

        Lies of P builds its feedback architecture entirely around the Perfect Guard — a system where the audio cue, visual flash, and haptic burst must be parsed faster than conscious thought. The design demands that players internalize the feedback loop to the point of unconscious execution, making mastery feel genuinely physical.

          Primary Modalities
        • Visual: Perfect Guard spark ring, Fury Attack red glow, boss phase bar drain, Fatal Attack prompt
        • Auditory: Metallic guard clang, Fury Attack deep boom, Fable Art activation tone
        • Haptic: Perfect Guard short burst, escalating impact rumble, Fatal Attack vibration

        Spatial Congruence in Guard-Centric Combat

        Lies of P achieves spatial congruence by making the guard mechanic directionally neutral — unlike many parry systems that require facing, P's Perfect Guard works in any direction. This eliminates the spatial translation cost of repositioning before guarding, allowing players to focus entirely on the temporal dimension of the interaction.

        Cognitive Load Bifurcation

        Lies of P manages cognitive load through a clear primary skill gate: the Perfect Guard. Rather than distributing player attention across multiple mechanics simultaneously, the system funnels all defensive attention into a single learnable pattern — reducing Extrinsic Load by making the interface's primary demand singular and consistent.

        Extrinsic Load Intrinsic Focus
        Perfect Guard as Primary Skill Gate

        The Perfect Guard creates a clear, learnable mastery curve. Players who invest in the mechanic feel genuine skill progression — the system rewards attention to enemy animation rather than reflex alone.
        Weapon Assembly Complexity

        The combination of blade and handle stats, elemental affinities, and Fable Arts creates a combinatorial space that can overwhelm players who want to engage with the system but lack a framework for evaluation.
        Fury Attack Multi-Sensory Warning

        Unblockable attacks are flagged by both visual (red glow) and audio (deep boom) — a well-designed multi-modal warning that gives players time to respond with appropriate input (dodge instead of guard).
        Boss Difficulty Spikes

        Phase 2 transitions often dramatically accelerate attack speeds without proportionate communication — the timing window reduction is discovered through failure rather than signaled, creating abrupt difficulty walls.
        Lies of P proves that a soulslike's HCI can be built around a single defensive mechanic without losing depth. The Perfect Guard is not a simplification — it is a focusing of the genre's core tension into one decisive interaction.

        Strengths and Weaknesses Analysis

        A comprehensive evaluation of Lies of P's interface paradigm — examining the tension between its celebrated Perfect Guard defensive innovation and the inherited opacity of the soulslike genre it both homages and refines.

        System Strengths Positive_Vectors System Weaknesses Negative_Vectors
        01_GUARD
        Perfect Guard Innovation

        The Perfect Guard is a genuine evolution of soulslike combat design — shifting the defensive paradigm from evasion to precision timing, creating a distinct combat identity for Lies of P within a crowded genre.
        01_TELEGRAPH
        Enemy Telegraph Inconsistency

        Some enemy attack animations are insufficiently telegraphed for the Perfect Guard timing they require — particularly imported Soulslike-style 'delayed swing' attacks that punish the learned timing pattern.
        02_WEAPON
        Weapon Assembly Creativity

        The blade/handle combination system creates a rich space for player expression — players develop personal weapon philosophies that make their builds feel uniquely authored.
        02_SPIKE
        Boss Difficulty Spikes

        Phase 2 transitions occasionally introduce abrupt difficulty escalations that feel like design rather than discovery — punishing players for mastering Phase 1 without adequate preparation for Phase 2 patterns.
        03_HUMANITY
        Narrative Choice Integration

        The lie/truth choice system integrates narrative into gameplay in a meaningful way — P's humanity affects available endings and NPC interactions, making dialogue choice a form of HCI.
        03_CLARITY
        Stat Opacity

        Weapon and Ergo stat interactions are insufficiently communicated in-game. Like the soulslike genre it draws from, external resources are often required to understand optimal build construction.

        Lies of P demonstrates that soulslike HCI can evolve without abandoning its core design values. The Perfect Guard is a refinement — not a simplification — of the genre's parry tradition, creating a more accessible skill ceiling than most FromSoftware titles while preserving the depth that makes mastery feel earned. Its weaknesses are largely inherited from its genre predecessors rather than introduced by its own design decisions.
