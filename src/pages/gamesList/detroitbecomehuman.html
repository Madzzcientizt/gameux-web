        Overview

        Detroit: Become Human is an interactive drama and action-adventure game developed by Quantic Dream and published by Sony Interactive Entertainment. Released on May 25, 2018, for PlayStation 4, and later on PC in 2019, the game is set in 2038 Detroit where human-like androids have become commonplace.

        Detroit's UI/UX centers on its revolutionary branching narrative flowchart system. After each chapter, players see a detailed decision tree visualizing every path, locked outcomes, and percentage of other players who made each choice. In-game, contextual prompts, QTE inputs, and environmental scanning use a sleek android-vision aesthetic reinforcing the game's themes of artificial consciousness.

        UI Components

        [ ELEMENT_01 ] The Diegetic Investigation HUD

        Detroit: Become Human employs a minimalist, diegetic HUD that integrates interface elements directly into the game world through the android protagonists' visual processing. Connor's crime scene analysis mode overlays holographic markers, reconstruction timelines, and evidence tags onto the 3D environment, treating the HUD as part of the character's android perception rather than an external game overlay.

          HUD Elements
        • Contextual Button Prompts (R2, Triangle, etc.)
        • Objective Markers & Mission Indicators
        • Dialogue Choice Wheel
        • Crime Scene Reconstruction Timeline
        [ ELEMENT_02 ] Environmental Scanning

        The R2-triggered scan mode is a core interface mechanic that highlights all interactable objects in the environment with glowing outlines and contextual button prompts. This creates a visual affordance layer where players can distinguish between passive scenery and interactive elements. The scan functions as a problem-solving tool during investigation sequences, surfacing hidden clues and reconstructing events.

        [ ELEMENT_03 ] Dialogue Choice System

        The dialogue system presents choices mapped to the controller's face buttons (Triangle, Circle, Cross, Square) in a radial layout anchored to the right side of the screen. Timed choices add urgency through a shrinking visual indicator, and some options are locked behind prior exploration or evidence gathering. A relationship tracker displays NPC disposition changes in real-time after each interaction.

        Fitt's Law in Contextual Interactions

        Fitt's Law applies to Detroit: Become Human through its contextual button prompts and Quick Time Events (QTEs). Interactive objects display button prompts when the player character is within a defined proximity radius (Distance D), and the prompt's visual target size (Width W) scales based on the interaction's urgency. During calm exploration, prompts are small and persistent; during action QTEs, they appear larger but with shorter time windows.

        [ MATHEMATICAL MODEL ]
        MT = a + b * log2(2D/W)

        The game's QTE design deliberately manipulates Fitt's Law variables: during high-tension chase sequences, the effective target width (W) is reduced through rapid button-prompt changes and directional swipe inputs, while the cognitive distance (D) increases as players must shift attention between on-screen action and prompt locations. This creates a controlled difficulty curve tied directly to narrative tension.

        Visual Hierarchy Breakdown

        Layering of Information
        1. Narrative Focus

          Primary focus. The cinematic camera, character expressions, and environmental storytelling dominate the player's visual attention. The near-invisible HUD ensures that narrative immersion is never broken by intrusive UI elements, keeping the player focused on the story.

        2. Interaction Prompts

          Secondary focus. Contextual button prompts appear directly on or near interactive objects in world-space, using the android's perspective as justification. These diegetic cues minimize the gap between game world and interface, reducing cognitive switching costs.

        3. System Feedback

          Tertiary focus. Relationship status notifications, chapter flowchart progress, and objective updates appear as brief overlays that fade quickly. The post-chapter flowchart provides strategic meta-context without interrupting real-time gameplay flow.

        User Interaction

        An examination of the multi-modal input and feedback systems within Detroit: Become Human's interactive drama framework, analyzing how player agency is expressed through branching choices, environmental interaction, and motion-controlled Quick Time Events across three interconnected protagonist narratives.

        Core Gameplay Loop

        1. Explore

          Players navigate 3D environments in third-person, scanning surroundings with R2 to reveal interactable objects, clues, and NPC dialogue options. Exploration depth directly unlocks additional choices in later decision points.

        2. Decide

          At critical story junctions, players are presented with timed dialogue choices and moral dilemmas mapped to controller buttons. Each choice modifies relationship meters, unlocks or locks future paths, and can permanently alter the narrative trajectory.

        3. React

          Action sequences employ QTE inputs — button presses, analog stick movements, touchpad swipes, and motion controls. Failed QTEs do not result in "game over" but instead branch the story into alternate consequences, including permanent character death.

        4. Reflect

          After each chapter, the flowchart system reveals the branching structure of all possible paths, showing which nodes the player reached and what percentage of other players chose each option. This drives replay motivation and narrative awareness.

        Multi-Modal Feedback Matrix

        Detroit: Become Human delivers feedback through a carefully synchronized triad of sensory channels. When a player makes a dialogue choice, successfully completes a QTE, or discovers a clue, the system responds with visual, auditory, and haptic signals simultaneously. This multi-modal approach ensures that the emotional weight of each decision is reinforced across all available sensory pathways.

        • Audio Feedback

          Detroit uses an adaptive orchestral soundtrack that shifts dynamically based on player choices and story tension. Each protagonist has a distinct musical identity — Connor's themes feature synthetic and procedural tones, Kara's use emotional piano and strings, and Markus's incorporate rising orchestral swells during revolutionary moments. Sound cues accompany relationship meter changes with subtle positive or negative audio stings.

        • Visual Feedback

          The game communicates consequences through on-screen relationship notifications (e.g., "Hank: Warm ↑"), environmental color grading shifts that reflect narrative tone, and the LED ring on android characters' temples — which cycles between blue (calm), yellow (processing), and red (stress). The post-chapter flowchart provides a comprehensive visual map of decisions made and paths not taken.

        • Input/Tactile Fidelity

          The DualShock 4 is used extensively as a narrative input device. The touchpad mimics in-game actions like swiping to clean surfaces or unlock doors. Motion controls require players to physically tilt or shake the controller during QTEs, creating a direct kinesthetic link between player gesture and character action. Haptic vibrations intensify during high-tension moments, such as chase sequences or life-or-death decisions.

        HCI Principles

        Cybernetic Feedback Loops

        Detroit: Become Human employs an open-loop narrative feedback architecture where player decisions propagate forward through a branching story graph with no ability to undo or reverse choices during a playthrough. The system's feedback loop operates across three timescales: immediate (QTE success/failure), medium-term (relationship meter shifts and unlocked dialogue), and long-term (chapter-ending flowchart reveals and permanent character death consequences).

          Primary Modalities
        • Visual: Diegetic android HUD, contextual button prompts, relationship status notifications, LED temple ring color states, flowchart decision maps
        • Auditory: Adaptive protagonist-specific soundtracks, relationship change audio stings, environmental narrative audio, QTE success/failure tones
        • Structural: Branching narrative flowchart, three-protagonist story interleaving, chapter-based progression, timed choice constraints

        Spatial Congruence

        Detroit: Become Human achieves strong spatial congruence by anchoring all UI elements within the game world through the android protagonists' visual processing capabilities. Button prompts appear physically attached to in-world objects rather than floating in screen-space. The R2 scan mode renders interactive highlights as part of the character's perception, not as an external overlay. This diegetic approach eliminates the cognitive dissonance between "game interface" and "game world," treating the entire UI as an extension of the androids' built-in heads-up display.

        Cognitive Load Bifurcation

        Detroit: Become Human carefully manages cognitive load by minimizing extrinsic interface complexity while maximizing intrinsic narrative and moral decision-making demands. The near-invisible HUD removes traditional gaming UI friction, while the branching story structure imposes a heavy cognitive burden through consequence-awareness, moral reasoning, and multi-protagonist narrative tracking.

        Extrinsic Organization Intrinsic Focus
        Diegetic Minimal HUD

        The game eliminates traditional HUD elements by embedding all interface cues within the android characters' visual processing. Prompts only appear when contextually relevant, reducing constant visual noise to near-zero during cinematic moments.

        Moral Decision Weight

        Players must evaluate ethical dilemmas with permanent, irreversible consequences — including character death. The knowledge that choices cannot be undone creates a persistent cognitive pressure that layers on top of the narrative engagement.

        Simplified Input Mapping

        Interactions are mapped to intuitive controller gestures — swipes for physical actions, button presses for dialogue, motion controls for effort. This gestural congruence reduces the learning curve by making inputs physically analogous to in-game actions.

        Multi-Protagonist Tracking

        Managing three interleaved protagonist storylines (Connor, Markus, Kara) with independent relationship meters, narrative states, and survival status demands continuous strategic awareness across parallel narrative threads.

        Detroit: Become Human's interface is not merely a control system — it is a narrative instrument. Its diegetic design proves that interactive dramas can achieve cinematic immersion without sacrificing player agency, by embedding every interface element within the fiction itself.

        Strengths and Weaknesses Analysis

        A comprehensive evaluation of Detroit: Become Human's interactive drama interface paradigm. Examining the tension between cinematic narrative immersion and player agency, and how the game's diegetic UI approach serves and limits the player experience across its branching story structure.

        System Strengths Positive_Vectors System Weaknesses Negative_Vectors
        01_IMMERSION
        Diegetic UI Integration

        The game achieves exceptional narrative immersion by embedding all interface elements within the android characters' visual processing. The near-invisible HUD eliminates the separation between "playing a game" and "inhabiting the story," creating a seamless cinematic experience that supports emotional engagement.

        01_QTE_FRUSTRATION
        QTE Input Friction

        The motion control and touchpad QTE inputs can feel imprecise and frustrating, especially during high-stakes action sequences. Misregistered swipes or accidental button presses can lead to unintended narrative consequences, and the lack of an undo or retry mechanism amplifies this frustration for players who value precise input.

        02_FLOWCHART
        Revolutionary Flowchart System

        The post-chapter flowchart is a landmark UI innovation in interactive narrative design. By visualizing the complete decision tree — including locked paths and global player statistics — it transforms opaque branching narratives into transparent, explorable systems, driving replay value and meta-narrative awareness.

        02_TIMED_CHOICES
        Aggressive Choice Timers

        Timed dialogue choices with irreversible consequences can create excessive pressure, particularly for players who want to thoughtfully engage with moral dilemmas. The timer does not distinguish between narrative urgency and genuine time pressure, occasionally forcing uninformed decisions that feel unfair rather than dramatically tense.

        03_CONSEQUENCE_DEPTH
        Meaningful Consequence Architecture

        Detroit's branching narrative delivers genuinely divergent outcomes — not cosmetic variations. Character death is permanent, entire storylines can be lost, and late-game events are shaped by dozens of accumulated micro-decisions. This creates one of the most consequential player-choice systems in gaming, validated by over 40 possible endings.

        03_EXPLORATION_LIMITS
        Constrained Environmental Agency

        Despite the illusion of open exploration, player movement is tightly confined to predetermined paths and small interactive zones. The camera is often fixed or semi-fixed, and the inability to freely explore the richly detailed world of 2038 Detroit can feel restrictive, undermining the sense of agency that the narrative choices promise.

        Detroit: Become Human's interface represents a benchmark in interactive narrative HCI — a system designed to deliver maximum narrative immersion with minimum interface friction. While its strengths lie in diegetic UI innovation, the revolutionary flowchart system, and deeply consequential choice architecture, its weaknesses reveal the inherent tension between cinematic control and player agency. The system's greatest achievement is proving that a game interface can become invisible, letting the story — and its moral weight — become the entire experience.
