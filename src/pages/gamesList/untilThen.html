        Overview

          Core Gameplay Loops:

        • Atmospheric Campus/City Navigation
        • Diegetic Smartphone OS Management
        • Rhythm & Mundane Minigames

        Until Then is a cinematic adventure game developed by Polychroma Games. Set in a fictionalized Philippines grappling with the emotional aftermath of a global tragedy, players follow high school student Mark Borja as he navigates shifting friendships, academic pressures, and a steadily unraveling reality. The game gamifies the experience of navigating an atmospheric campus mystery, leaning heavily on deep cultural aesthetics and a haunting, narrative-driven piano soundtrack to set the tone.

        From an HCI perspective, the game is a beautiful exploration of diegetic UI. Rather than relying on traditional text boxes or abstract dialogue wheels, the bulk of the storytelling and interaction happens through a fully simulated, minimalist smartphone operating system. By mimicking the modern digital habits of scrolling, typing, and managing notifications, the game bridges the gap between the character's cognitive load and the player's own real-world muscle memory.

        • Cognitive Load
          Emotional / Social
          Parsing context through the speed of text replies, deleted drafts, and social media feed curation.
        • Interaction Frequency
          Conversational Rhythm
          Requires rhythmic key presses during piano minigames and intentional pacing when simulating typing messages.
        • Spatial Targeting
          2.5D Parallax
          Moving through deeply layered, familiar environments that serve as the physical interface for storytelling.

        UI Components

        [ ELEMENT_01 ] The Diegetic Smartphone

        The absolute core of the UI. Players open a simulated phone to access messaging apps, a fictionalized Facebook ("Ruler"), and photo galleries. It utilizes a calm, minimalist aesthetic that feels inherently familiar, serving as the central hub for exposition and character relationship building.

          Functionality
        • Infinite scrolling mechanics simulating modern doomscrolling
        • Read receipts and typing indicators acting as tension builders
        [ ELEMENT_02 ] Piano Minigame HUD

        To break up the narrative walking simulation, the game occasionally shifts into rhythm-based mechanics, specifically during piano practice. Notes cascade down the screen, requiring synchronized keystrokes to maintain the musical flow, providing an interactive dimension to the game's stellar, narrative-driven soundtrack.

        [ ELEMENT_03 ] Spatial Prompts

        When exploring the school campus or taking the LRT, the UI remains hidden. Interaction points (like a poster or a classmate) are designated by subtle, contextual button prompts that organically hover over the 2.5D pixel art without shattering the cinematic immersion.

        Typing Simulation & Social Friction

        A fascinating UX choice in *Until Then* is how it gamifies the act of texting. Instead of just clicking "Send Reply," the player must repeatedly press a button to "type out" the message character by character.

        [ EMOTIONAL UX ]
        Friction = Hesitation / Revision

        This simple mechanical friction forces the player to engage with the *cadence* of the conversation. The game will often have Mark type out a harsh or overly emotional response, only for him to stop, backspace the entire sentence, and type a more neutral reply. The UI forces the player to literally perform his social hesitation, turning the text box into an engine for empathy.

        Visual Hierarchy Breakdown

        Diegetic Layering
        1. Foreground (The Screen)

          The physical smartphone bezel and its illuminated UI. When the phone is out, the depth of field shifts, completely blurring the background to simulate the tunnel vision of looking at a screen.

        2. Midground (The Subject)

          Mark and the interactive elements of the room or campus. High-contrast pixel art separates interactable objects from the static background scenery.

        3. Background (The Atmosphere)

          Lush, parallax-scrolling backdrops of familiar cityscapes and school hallways, providing spatial context and establishing the distinct "vibe" of a rainy afternoon commute.

        The seamless transition between managing the smartphone UI and walking through the 2.5D world accurately captures the modern cognitive state: a constant, fluid balancing act between our physical environment and our digital lives.

        User Interaction

        An analysis of how *Until Then* uses mechanics to turn mundane daily routines into engaging narrative beats.

        The Interaction Loop

        1. Navigate

          Traverse the linear 2.5D environments, soaking in the environmental storytelling of the campus and city transit.

        2. Socialize (Digital)

          Pull up the smartphone to check the social feed, "like" posts, and simulate the act of typing out carefully curated responses to friends.

        3. Perform (Minigames)

          Engage in sudden mechanical shifts—like rhythm-based piano playing, inserting a USB drive, or buying fishball from a street vendor.

        4. Reflect

          Process the shifting, glitching realities of the mystery as the UI itself begins to subtly alter alongside Mark's psychological state.

        Multi-Modal Feedback Matrix

        Because the game relies heavily on everyday actions, the sensory feedback must make those actions feel weighty and significant. The sound of a chat bubble popping up is just as crucial as the visual lighting of the scene.

        • Auditory Feedback

          The familiar ping of a notification, the rhythmic clatter of a mechanical keyboard, and the ambient noise of a busy school cafeteria ground the digital UI elements firmly within the physical world of the narrative.

        • Visual Glitches (The Déjà Vu)

          As the overarching mystery deepens, the screen utilizes intentional screen-tearing and UI distortion. Characters might glitch out for a fraction of a second, merging the pristine pixel art with abrasive digital corruption to signal timeline shifts.

        • Tactile Pacing

          The game dictates emotional pacing through mechanical requirements. Scrolling slowly through a depressing timeline feed feels different than frantically tapping the button to sprint through a rain-soaked hallway.

        HCI Principles

        Spatial Congruence & Cultural Familiarity

        For a user interface or environment to be truly immersive, it must map to the user's mental models. *Until Then* excels at capturing the spatial and cultural congruence of a high school campus and city transit system. The layout of the classrooms, the placement of the club rooms, and the chaotic geometry of the streets act as an invisible, intuitive map that immediately feels lived-in.

          Cultural Signifiers
        • Environmental Anchors: Familiar landmarks and commute routes (like riding the train) give the 2D space a 3D structural logic.
        • Diegetic Menus: The UI does not feel like a "game menu"; it feels like holding a real phone in a real school hallway.

        The Illusion of Social Media

        The game builds a remarkably robust simulation of social media. Players can "like" posts, share comments, and observe the fluctuating follower counts of their classmates. By utilizing a UI format that players already engage with for hours every day in real life, the game completely bypasses the standard "tutorial phase" of teaching a player how to navigate menus. The muscle memory is already installed in the user.

        Cinematic Presentation vs. Readability

        Balancing beautiful, cinematic pixel art with the necessity of clear, readable text interfaces is a constant challenge in narrative games.

        Extrinsic Organization Intrinsic Focus
        Depth of Field Blur

        When the phone is active, the complex pixel-art background softens dramatically. This immediately reduces visual noise, allowing the stark, minimalist text of the UI to stand out.

        Environmental Distraction

        Because the world is so dense with animated details (rain, background characters, moving trains), players must actively focus to find the subtle interaction prompts.

        Speech Bubbles

        Instead of a static text box locked to the bottom of the screen, dialogue bubbles float dynamically near the characters' heads, guiding the player's eyes to the action in the scene rather than away from it.

        Reading Speed

        The pacing of the text reveal matches natural conversational rhythms, preventing the player from skipping ahead and breaking the cinematic tension of the scene.

        By anchoring its most critical story beats inside the glowing rectangle of a smartphone, *Until Then* leverages the very real psychological weight we already attach to our notifications, turning a simple UI tool into a powerful narrative device.

        Strengths and Weaknesses Analysis

        Evaluating the friction between an incredibly immersive, culturally resonant presentation and the mechanical limitations of the adventure genre.

        System Strengths Positive_Vectors System Weaknesses Negative_Vectors
        01_IMMERSION
        Diegetic Brilliance

        The integration of the smartphone OS and the seamless transition into piano rhythm minigames keeps the player constantly engaged with the world, avoiding the disconnect of abstract pause menus.

        01_TRAVERSAL_FATIGUE
        Slow Pacing

        The commitment to cinematic realism means Mark's walking speed is locked to a relatively slow, deliberate pace. Backtracking through long stretches of campus can occasionally frustrate players seeking faster progression.

        02_AESTHETIC
        Emotional Resonance

        By utilizing a clean, calm, and minimalist aesthetic for the digital interfaces contrasted against the beautifully lit, chaotic pixel-art environments, the game achieves a distinct and powerful visual identity.

        02_CLARITY
        Prompt Visibility

        Because the interaction UI prompts are designed to be unintrusive and blend seamlessly with the environment, players can sometimes walk past crucial interactive objects without noticing them against the dense backgrounds.

        *Until Then* succeeds by transforming the mundane into the profound. By utilizing a diegetic smartphone interface and a beautifully structured 2.5D campus, it leverages our real-world digital habits to draw us deeper into its atmospheric, narrative-driven mystery.
