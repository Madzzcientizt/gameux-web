        Overview

          Sekai Architecture:

        • Custom Unity Engine
        • Colorful Palette

        Hatsune Miku: Colorful Stage! (Project Sekai, 2020) is a mobile rhythm game. Unlike arcade or PC rhythm games that rely on tactile hardware feedback (keyboard switches or arcade buttons), mobile rhythm games must conquer the "Flat Glass" problem.

        Project Sekai provides an excellent study in touchscreen ergonomics and dynamic lane scaling. Instead of fixed vertical tracks, the playfield actively curves and scales in 3D depth, guiding the user's thumbs naturally toward the strike zone.

        • Cognitive Load
          High
          Scaling multi-lane reading disguised as a 3D perspective.
        • Response Time
          ± 40ms
          Heavily bound by the polling rate of the capacitive touch screen.
        • Tactile Load
          Zero
          Total lack of physical button resistance.

        UI Components

        [ ELEMENT_01 ] Curved Judgement Line

        To accommodate the radial sweep of human thumbs holding a physical smartphone, the judgement line is subtly curved, increasing hit accuracy at screen edges.

        [ ELEMENT_02 ] Directional Flick Arrows

        Specialized gestures (Flicks) require the user to drag fingers across the glass, adding kinesthetic energy to the otherwise flat interaction format.

        [ ELEMENT_03 ] Procedural Skill Indicators

        Flashcards appear on the left/right periphery to indicate active character abilities (score boosts, life recovery). These are designed with high contrast and distinct sound-barks to ensure players register the 'state update' without looking away from the falling notes.

        [ ELEMENT_04 ] Variable Width Lane Bars

        Notes vary from 1-lane to 6-lane widths dynamically. This uses visual weighting to signify note importance and input leniency, allowing for more expressive choreographic gesture mapping than fixed-column VSRGs.

        User Interaction

        The UX flow is built specifically for capacitive glass, mitigating the lack of tactile hardware feedback through kinesthetic empathy.

        The Capacitive Loop

        1. Read Depth

          Anticipate 3D notes emerging from the vanishing point in scaled perspective.

        2. Tap/Flick

          Execute glass strikes or violent swipe vectors (resetting thumb position).

        3. Trace

          Follow green curved hold-paths, mapping physical movement to melodic glissandos.

        4. Validate

          The "Fat Finger" offset pop-up appears ABOVE the thumb to ensure the feedback loop is never occluded.

        Multi-Modal Feedback Matrix

        Sekai uses mobile haptics and visual offsets to bridge the 'Flat Glass' tactile gap.

        • Synthetic Haptic Rhythms

          The phone vibration motor triggers on Flick notes, synthetically replacing the missing mechanical switch feedback for high-impact inputs.

        • MV Background Dimming

          Users can adjust 3D-Live transparency, allowing them to balance cinematic spectacle (High MV) with competitive readability (Low MV).

        HCI Principles

        Finger Occlusion & Offset Correction

        A critical HCI failure in mobile apps is 'Anatomical Occlusion' where the finger hides the UI. Sekai solves this by rendering the judgement pop-up (PERFECT/GREAT) 200px ABOVE the judgement line, ensuring the feedback enters the user's foveal vision without being blocked by their physical thumb.

        Strengths and Weaknesses Analysis

        System Strengths Positive_Vectors System Weaknesses Negative_Vectors
        01_ERGONOMICS
        Biomechanical Synergy

        The curved hit line completely acknowledges the physical limitations of reaching thumbs stretching across modern 6+ inch phone screens.
        01_HARDWARE
        Polling Rate Friction

        Low-end Android phones with 60Hz polling limits will randomly drop inputs, completely severing the trust between user intent and system reaction.
        02_FLEXIBILITY
        Variable Width Tuning

        Allowing a single note to visually span 5 lanes ensures casual players never miss it, while keeping strict timing windows for pros.
        02_OCCLUSION
        Anatomical Occlusion

        Despite offsets, playing with 4 fingers (index-style playing on a tablet) heavily obscures the lower-third of the screen, creating massive blind spots.
        03_FEEDBACK
        Synthetic Haptics

        Triggering the phone's internal vibration motor precisely on "Flick" notes synthetically replaces the missing mechanical switch feel.
        03_FRICTION
        Glass Moisture Drag

        Continuous "Hold and Swipe" mechanics fail frequently if the user's fingers are sweaty, as kinetic friction destroys capacitive dragging.

        Project Sekai expertly mitigates the innate flaws of capacitive touch screens—lack of haptics, anatomical occlusion, and fat-finger errors—by utilizing dynamic width scaling and synthetic vibration feedback to craft a tactile rhythm experience on flat glass.
