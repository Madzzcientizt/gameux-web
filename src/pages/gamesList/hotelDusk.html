        Overview

        The narrative unfolds over the course of a single night in 1979 at the titular Hotel Dusk, a fading establishment rumored to house a room that can grant wishes. As Kyle Hyde, players navigate the hotel's halls to fulfill his job as a "fetcher" for Red Crown, but the mission quickly becomes personal as he discovers that every guest—and the hotel itself—is connected to his past. Through a series of deep, interrogation-style conversations, players must carefully choose their words to pry secrets from the eccentric cast, uncovering a web of crime, betrayal, and lost dreams that leads back to Kyle's disgraced departure from the police force.

        Gameplay is deeply tactile, utilizing the DS hardware to ground the player in Kyle's world through various touch-screen puzzles and environmental interactions. From physically turning the console to "flip" a painting to using the stylus to pick locks or piece together torn letters, the mechanics emphasize the grounded, gritty nature of the noir genre. The experience is defined by its moody, jazz-fusion soundtrack and a slow-burn script that values character development over flashy action, ultimately delivering a poignant story about human regret and the possibility of a second chance.

        UI Components

        [ ELEMENT_01 ] Character Portrait & Dialogue Mode

        The primary interface for storytelling and interrogation. It features high-contrast, "sketch-style" character portraits with animated line work to convey emotion and tone during conversations.

          Visual Feedback
        • Hand-drawn charcoal aesthetic with subtle "jitter" animations.
        • Dynamic backgrounds that blur to keep focus on the speaker.
        • White outlines around character sprites to separate them from the environment.
        [ ELEMENT_02 ] Interactive Sketchbook

        A creative tool that allows players to draw or take notes directly on the screen using the stylus. It mimics a physical notepad, providing a space for solving puzzles or recording clues found during the investigation.

        [ ELEMENT_03 ] Item Inventory & Description

        A dual-screen interface used for managing personal belongings. The right screen lists items in a notebook format, while the left screen displays a 3D render of the selected object along with a narrative description.

        [ ELEMENT_04 ] Exploration Map & HUD

        A top-down perspective used for navigating environments. It features a stylized floor plan with icons for movement and interaction, alongside a dedicated toolbar for quick access to core game functions.

        Visual Hierarchy Breakdown

        Layering of Narrative Information
        1. Interactive Narrative Cues

          The highest level of hierarchy. During dialogue, the UI generates a pulsing "exclamation" icon or a "Talk" button when a breakthrough is possible. These use high-contrast yellow and red highlights that "pop" against the monochromatic, rotoscoped character sketches. This visual tension alerts the player to critical branching paths in the investigation, demanding immediate cognitive focus to progress the story.

        2. Environmental Investigation

          The mid-level hierarchy focuses on spatial scaffolding. When Kyle Hyde enters "Investigate" mode, the screen presents a 3D view paired with a 2D map. Taggable "hotspots" are often subtle until hovered over, but the map icons remain highly saturated to guide the player's "line" of inquiry. This ensures players always have a clear directional path through the hotel's corridors without breaking the gritty, noir atmosphere.

        3. Diegetic Inventory & Ledger

          Peripheral data is tucked away into Kyle's notebook. The Item Inventory and personal notes are presented as a literal physical object within the game world. Accessing these requires a deliberate shift in attention, forcing the player to "step back" from the immediate environment to synthesize clues. This reinforces the detective fantasy, where checking your status and evidence is a quiet, contemplative act performed in the "pockets" between intense character encounters.

        User Interaction

        An examination of the investigative and diagnostic paradigms in Hotel Dusk: Room 215 UI design, focusing on how the game communicates mystery-solving systems and player status through layered feedback mechanisms. The game employs a multi-modal feedback matrix that integrates visual, auditory, and haptic cues to create a tactile noir experience. Players must interpret these cues in real-time—often holding the Nintendo DS vertically like a book—to navigate the hotel's secrets and handle high-stakes interrogations.

        Core Gameplay Loop

        Investigation-Interrogation Cycle
        1. Roam

          Navigate Hotel Dusk's corridors using the touch-screen map, searching for environmental clues and interactive hotspots within the 3D space.

        2. Examine

          Investigate specific objects or documents using the stylus to manipulate, clean, or move items to uncover hidden details or codes.

        3. Probe

          Engage in deep conversations with hotel guests, using "interruption" icons to challenge inconsistencies and push the narrative forward.

        4. Solve

          Synthesize clues at the end of each chapter through a summary quiz, finalizing Kyle Hyde's thoughts to progress into the next hour of the night.

        Multi-Modal Feedback Matrix

        Hotel Dusk: Room 215 employs low-key, atmospheric sensory feedback to keep the player in a "detective flow state." Successful narrative breakthroughs receive subtle audio-visual reinforcement, while making a social faux pas or being kicked out of the hotel triggers immediate shifts in the sensory environment, including a "Game Over" screen featuring Kyle leaving the hotel.

        • Audio Feedback

          Atmospheric jazz and moody synth transitions define the auditory landscape. Audio cues include the scratch of the stylus in the sketchbook, the distinct "chime" when a new clue is added to the ledger, and the tense, quickening tempo when a character is hiding the truth.

        • Visual Feedback

          A unique "pencil-sketch" rotoscoped aesthetic provides visual dynamism. Characters jitter with hand-drawn energy to convey emotion. When a critical contradiction is found, the screen may flash or zoom, and the "yellow triangle" interruption icon pulses to provide immediate feedback on narrative opportunities.

        • Haptic Feedback

          The Nintendo DS's vibration is utilized to signify environmental collisions or heavy doors closing. More importantly, the hardware interaction—such as closing the DS lid to "stamp" a piece of paper—serves as a physical extension of the UI's puzzle-solving logic.

        HCI Principles

        Cybernetic Feedback Loops

        Hotel Dusk: Room 215 implements a discovery-driven feedback architecture where player investigation decisions create immediate narrative consequences. The feedback loop operates on a Query-Response Cycle, with the execution of timed "interruptions" and environmental inspections serving as the critical junction point for uncovering the truth behind Room 215.

          Primary Modalities
        • Visual: Dynamic pencil-sketch animations, shifting character expressions, and the pulsing highlight of "hotspots" during investigation.
        • Auditory: Moody jazz shifts, the tactile "scratch" of the stylus on the sketchbook, and the heartbeat-like thumping during high-tension interrogations.
        • Structural: Narrative progression based on the successful extraction of information and the logical linking of inventory items.

        Spatial Congruence

        The interface maintains spatial consistency by utilizing the DS's dual screens to bridge the gap between 2D navigation and 3D exploration. Kyle's Notebook serves as a persistent spatial metaphor, allowing players to bridge the gap between world-traversal and internal thought (inventory and notes) using consistent interaction patterns. The "Book Style" orientation creates a direct physical mapping between the player's grip on the console and the act of a detective holding a case file.

        Strengths and Weaknesses Analysis

        A comprehensive evaluation of the UI/UX design in Hotel Dusk: Room 215, highlighting the innovative use of the dual-screen "Book Style" orientation and the challenges posed by its experimental touch-based investigation mechanics.

        System Strengths Positive_Vectors System Weaknesses Negative_Vectors
        01_IMMERSION
        Tactile Diegetic Integration

        By forcing the player to hold the DS vertically, the hardware itself becomes a diegetic prop—a detective's notebook. The interface leverages this by allowing players to physically "close" the console to solve puzzles or use the stylus for sketching, creating a seamless bond between player action and Kyle Hyde's investigation.

        01_FRICTION
        Input Ambiguity in Puzzles

        Some touch-screen interactions lack precise feedback, leading to "pixel hunting" during environmental examinations. Without clear signifiers for every interactable object, players may experience frustration when a logical solution fails due to a slightly misaligned stylus tap.

        02_AESTHETIC
        Evocative Noir Stylization

        The "pencil-sketch" rotoscoped animation style provides high visual contrast that guides the eye toward emotional cues. This cohesive aesthetic identity reinforces the gritty, 1970s mystery atmosphere, making the UI feel like an extension of the game's narrative tone rather than a separate layer.

        02_NAVIGATION
        Abstract Map Constraints

        The 2D top-down map on the touch screen is functional but minimalist. In multi-room sequences or when navigating the hotel's lobby, the lack of landmark detail can make spatial orientation difficult, requiring players to constantly toggle between the 3D view and the map to confirm their position.

        03_NARRATIVE
        Dynamic Interrogation Feedback

        The "Interruption" system provides immediate mechanical feedback for narrative breakthroughs. The visual cues (vibrant yellow triangles) combined with the sudden shift in character animation create a satisfying "climax" in dialogue, reinforcing the player's role as a sharp, attentive investigator.

        03_ACCESSIBILITY
        Ergonomic Fatigue

        The vertical "Book Style" grip, while immersive, can cause physical discomfort during long play sessions for certain users. The lack of an option to mirror the UI for left-handed players in all versions, or the constant need to hold the stylus while supporting the console's weight, presents significant ergonomic barriers.

        The interface for Hotel Dusk: Room 215 prioritizes narrative immersion by embedding investigative tools into a tactile, diegetic "handheld notebook" experience. By utilizing the unique dual-screen hardware of the DS and a charcoal-sketch aesthetic, the system bridges the gap between the player's physical touch and Kyle Hyde's mental process. This design ensures that players feel like active participants in a noir novel, where every tap of the stylus carries the weight of a detective's intuition.
